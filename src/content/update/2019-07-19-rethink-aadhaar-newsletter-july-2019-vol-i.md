Resistance to Aadhaar will not end with enrolment. We hope that it will strengthen the struggle for many of us who had earlier felt safe not being enrolled and will make the struggle even more imperative.</p><p>If you are looking for options to stay out of the system and are worried about the security and privacy risks of the Aadhaar project, we urge you to consult a lawyer. You could also seek legal advice on the potential penalties or other consequences of, for instance, a delayed tax return filing. It would be best to take an informed decision specific to your situation. In case you'd like us to connect you with a lawyer, please write to <a href="mailto:legal@rethinkaadhaar.in" target="_blank">legal@rethinkaadhaar.in</a>.</p><h2>Aadhaar Amendments Bill passed by both Houses of Parliament</h2><p>The Aadhaar Amendments Bill, 2019, was passed by the Lok Sabha on July 4, after a day-long debate which involved fiery critique of the Bill from MPs NK Premachandran (RPI), Mahua Moitra (TMC), Supriya Sule (NCP), Ritesh Pandey (BSP), Asaduddin Owaisi (AIMIM), among others. Subsequently, the Bill was passed by Rajya Sabha on July 8. The Bill is identical to the version passed by the Lok Sabha in January, and later promulgated as an Ordinance, although during its final passage the government also promised to include schemes paid out of the Consolidated Fund of states. Read our statement on the Bill <a href="http://rethinkaadhaar.in/blog/2019/1/1/government-violating-sc-orders-by-introducing-amendments-for-commercial-use-of-aadhaar" target="_blank">here</a>. You can also read the full discussion of the Bill in the Lok Sabha at <a href="https://www.medianama.com/2019/07/223-live-aadhaar-amendment-bill-being-discussed-how-can-asks-for-private-info-without-data-protection-bill-asks-mahua-moitra/" target="_blank">MediaNama</a>.</p><h2>Rethink Aadhaar statement on Economic Survey</h2><p>The government’s vision to collate comprehensive, exhaustive data about every citizen and making it available for commercial use by private entities as stated in the budget document the Economic Survey 2018-19 is quite alarming. Read our <a href="http://rethinkaadhaar.in/blog/2019/7/6/budgets-economic-survey-pushes-for-interlinking-and-selling-citizens-data-for-private-purposes" target="_blank">full statement</a>.</p><h2>Delhi HC asks ECI to consider plea on Aadhaar-Voter ID linking</h2><p>A Delhi High Court bench headed by Chief Justice DN Patel directed the Election Commission of India (ECI) to <a href="https://www.livelaw.in/news-updates/pil-seeking-linking-of-voter-id-with-aadhaar-146441" target="_blank">consider a plea</a> by BJP leader Ashwini Upadhyay seeking linking of Aadhaar number with Voter IDs. The petitioner contends this will facilitate an “e-voting system using fingerprint and face biometric” which will “lead to an increase in voting percentage” and prevent “prevent bogus or fabricated voting”.
