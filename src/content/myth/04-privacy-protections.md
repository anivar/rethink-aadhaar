fact: "The Aadhaar Act allows authorities to share your personal data in the 'interest of national security' — a term that is left undefined."

The law permits sharing of identity information and authentication records under the undefined banner of "national security." Disclosure may occur on direction from a Joint Secretary‑ranked officer.

Section 33(1) allows demographic disclosure following a district judge's order, with prior hearing given to the UIDAI — but not to the affected person. Section 33(2) permits "national security" disclosures for three‑month periods, renewable indefinitely without ever notifying the user. Comparable democracies (e.g. Canada) cap surveillance duration and mandate notification within 90 days of cessation.

Aadhaar lacks provisions for breach notification, third‑party data‑use notices, or change‑of‑purpose notifications — all recommended by the 2012 Justice Shah Committee on Privacy.
