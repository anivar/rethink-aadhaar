myth: "Aadhaar is based fully on consent."
fact: "You may have to compulsorily enrol under Aadhaar, despite your privacy concerns."
---

Citizens often have no option but to obtain Aadhaar when frontline service agents demand it. The Aadhaar Act lacks any categorical language ensuring voluntariness; instead it broadens Aadhaar's scope. Section 7 extends mandatory linkage to any "subsidy, benefit or service" funded by the government treasury.

Recent notifications have required Aadhaar from trafficked women seeking assistance and from schoolchildren accessing meals. The Act's promise of "alternate and viable means" has been circumvented in practice — a "proof of applying for enrolment" is treated as the alternative.

Because the law also permits private companies to use Aadhaar, sharing your number with an employer or landlord may not constitute free consent in any meaningful sense.
