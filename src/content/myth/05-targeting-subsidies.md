fact: "Aadhaar was opened up for use by private companies — airlines, telecom, insurance, real estate — well beyond welfare delivery."

Congress MP Rajeev Satav questioned Clause 57 permitting private entities to use Aadhaar; Finance Minister Arun Jaitley did not address the question. UIDAI founder Nandan Nilekani spoke as early as 2012 about creating a "thriving application system" for the public and private sectors.

UIDAI entered into agreements with private companies before parliamentary passage of the Act. The Rajya Sabha moved to drop Clause 57's private‑authentication provision entirely. Even while the Bill was still in Parliament, private companies were openly advertising commercial access to citizens' Aadhaar data.
