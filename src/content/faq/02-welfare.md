question: "Does welfare actually need Aadhaar?"
short: "Welfare needs Aadhaar like a fish needs a bicycle."
---

The claim that Aadhaar is essential to welfare delivery has been repeated so often that it is taken for granted. But evidence on the ground suggests the opposite: every additional layer of biometric authentication and database seeding creates new failure modes — fingerprint mismatches, name spelling differences, lost numbers, network outages — and each failure mode produces real exclusions from rations, pensions, scholarships, and maternity benefits.

Pre‑existing welfare systems based on physical entitlements (ration cards, job cards, pension lists) worked, with their own problems, for decades. Aadhaar has not solved those problems; it has added new ones, while concentrating decision‑making power away from the citizen.
